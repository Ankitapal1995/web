TB-AMRpred: AI/ML-Based Drug Resistance Prediction

This project aimed to predict antimicrobial resistance in Mycobacterium tuberculosis using whole-genome sequencing (WGS) data without relying on predefined resistance markers. Machine learning and deep learning models were developed using XGBoost and artificial neural networks (ANN) and validated on independent datasets. SHAP-based explainable AI was applied to interpret model predictions and identify both known and novel resistance-associated mutations. The models achieved strong predictive performance (90–95% sensitivity and 94–99% specificity across 13 antitubercular drugs) and were implemented as a standalone analytical tool using Python and Bash, with deployment via a web server built using Python Flask and Apache for end-user access.

Single-Cell RNA-Seq Analysis of Drug Resistance Biomarkers in Breast Cancer

This project aimed to identify cell type–specific biomarkers underlying erlotinib resistance in breast cancer using single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq). Forty human breast cancer samples (20 erlotinib-resistant and 20 drug-sensitive) were analyzed using a standardized scRNA-seq workflow implemented in Seurat, with Harmony applied for batch correction and CellTypist used for cell type annotation. Marker gene identification and gene set enrichment analysis (GSEA) revealed enrichment of proliferation and metabolism-related pathways (MYC, E2F, oxidative phosphorylation) in drug-sensitive samples, while drug-resistant samples showed activation of inflammatory, survival, and EMT-associated pathways (TNF-α/NF-κB, JAK/STAT, hypoxia), highlighting distinct transcriptional programs associated with therapeutic response and resistance.

DraVAR: Variant Analysis and Resistance Biomarker Discovery Using Statistical-Based Methods

DraVAR is a Nextflow-based pipeline (also implemented in Bash) for analyzing whole genome sequencing (WGS) data to identify variants associated with drug resistance in Mycobacterium tuberculosis. It integrates well known tools for quality control (FastQC, Trimmomatic), alignment (BWA-MEM), variant calling (Samtools, bcftools), and annotation (SnpEff). Using WHO-recommended statistical methods, mutations are graded by confidence levels (high, moderate, low association) based on prevalence and functional impact. The pipeline achieves over 80% accuracy against the gold standard mutation catalogues.

OncoVAR: Cancer-Specific Somatic Variant Detection

OncoVAR is a custom pipeline for detecting cancer-specific somatic mutations from matched tumor-normal Next Generation Sequencing (NGS) data. It employs Samtools and bcftools for variant calling, benchmarks against Mutect2 and VarScan2, and validates findings using a reference gene list to ensure accurate identification of tumor-exclusive mutations. Notably, OncoVAR identified additional mutations in EGFR, CDKN2A, TP53, and MED12 genes missed by Mutect2 and VarScan2, demonstrating enhanced sensitivity in mutation detection.

InTGS: Tuberculosis Genomic Surveillance Platform

A genomic pipeline was developed to predict antibiotic resistance from tuberculosis patient samples. It was deployed as part of the Indian Tuberculosis Genomic Surveillance Consortium (InTGS), an initiative of the Indian government that collaborates with 10 prominent Indian institutes. The pipeline analyzes drug resistance variants in Indian tuberculosis patients to support TB-free goals of India.

MutMapTB: Protein Structure-Guided Functional Interpretation of Resistance-Associated Mutations

In silico functional analysis was conducted on newly discovered mutations associated with antitubercular drug resistance. The mechanistic basis of resistance was examined by mapping mutations onto 3D structures of key drug targets, measuring their proximity to drug binding sites, and comparing their abundance in drug-resistant versus susceptible Mycobacterium tuberculosis strains. A database was established to store mutation abundance data from resistant and susceptible isolates, along with their structural information and visualization.

Type 1 Diabetes Methylation Biomarker Discovery (AI/ML based and Statistical based)

NGS bisulfite sequencing data were analyzed to identify CpG site methylation patterns associated with Type 1 diabetes detection. Clinical cohort data from American and Chinese patients were examined using statistical, machine learning, and deep learning based methods to discover methylation-based biomarkers for Type 1 diabetes.

SnRNA-seq Bulk RNA-seq, Spatial Transcriptomics, and Metabolomics analysis

SnRNA-seq (single nuclear), Bulk RNA-seq, Spatial transcriptomics, and Metabolomics analyses were performed on several liver disease and healthy samples as part of a multi-modal data assessment for client deliverables. Bulk RNA-seq analysis was used to evaluate gene expression patterns, while spatial transcriptomics analysis using Squidpy provided spatial characterization of transcriptomic signals. Metabolomics analysis was conducted using in-house scripts and MetaboAnalyst to support validation of sample metadata and analytical consistency across datasets, ensuring reliable interpretation for downstream reporting.
